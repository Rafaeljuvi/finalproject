Falcon 1

Overview

Height : 70m

Diameter : 3.7m

Mass: 549,054kg

Falcon 1 is a reusable, two-stage rocket designed and manufactured by SpaceX for the reliable and safe transport of people and payloads into Earth orbit and beyond. Falcon 1 is the world's first orbital class reusable rocket. Reusability allows SpaceTD to re-fly the most expensive parts of the rocket, which in turn drives down the cost of space access. The Falcon 1 consists of two stages. The first stage, also known as the "booster," is powered by nine Merlin engines. The engines use rocket-grade kerosene (RP-1) as fuel and liquid oxygen (LOX) as oxidizer. The second stage, known as the "upper stage," is powered by a single Merlin Vacuum (MVac) engine optimized for the vacuum of space.

Dragon

Overview

Height : 8.1m

Diameter : 4m

Capsule Volume: 9.3mÂ³

The Dragon spacecraft is capable of carrying up to 7 passengers to and from Earth orbit, and beyond. It is the only spacecraft currently flying that is capable of returning significant amounts of cargo to Earth, and is the first private spacecraft to take humans to the space station. Both Dragon 1 and Dragon 2 are designed to be reused multiple times, significantly reducing the cost of space missions. After returning to Earth, Dragon capsules undergo refurbishment and can be flown again on future missions. SpaceX has been continuously improving the Dragon spacecraft based on its experience and customer feedback. The company plans to introduce upgraded versions of the Dragon spacecraft, including an enhanced version of Cargo Dragon for NASA's CRS-2 (Commercial Resupply Services 2) contract.

Falcon Heavy

Overview

Height : 70m

Diameter : 12.2m

Mass: 1,420,788kg

Falcon Heavy is composed of three reusable Falcon 9 nine-engine cores whose 27 Merlin engines together generate more than 5 million pounds of thrust at liftoff, equal to approximately eighteen 747 aircraft. As one of the world's most powerful operational rockets, Falcon Heavy can lift nearly 64 metric tons (141,000 lbs) to orbit.
